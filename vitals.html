Low grip strength can predict increased risk of functional limitations and complications with aging.

Strong grip and good muscle strength can help improve your quality of life.

Body Mass Index

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measurement used to assess whether a person is overweight or underweight, which can increase the risk of developing diseases.

As the BMI measurement is only based on weight and height, a better way to assess a person's health status is to take into account several factors, such as calorie intake and expenditure, metabolic rate, and body fat percentage.

It is also important to have a good balance between calorie intake and expenditure, and to increase one's metabolic rate through physical activity and exercise.

Lung function

Lung function measurement provides relevant information about the function and capacity of the lungs by measuring the speed of airflow and the amount of air that can be exhaled.

Your result is adjusted for your age, height, and gender and is expressed as a percentage. 100% indicates that the value is normal, while 50% indicates that the values are reduced to half of the normal value.

Waist/Hip ratio

FEV1/FVC ratio: calculated to compare lung size with the degree of airway opening. If this ratio is below 0.7 (or 70%), it indicates airway narrowing, also called airway obstruction.

You cannot improve your lung function through exercise, but you can improve your fitness level, muscle strength, and endurance, which can improve your breathing because your lungs will be less burdened during physical activity.

Always remember that your lung function test results do not necessarily reflect what you can physically do in your daily life. It depends on your fitness level and muscle strength, among other factors. Therefore, it is important to stay physically active regardless of whether your lung function is normal or reduced.

If you smoke and quit smoking, you may experience a slight increase in your lung function. After quitting smoking, you will only lose lung function at the same rate as people who have never smoked.
