Lipoprotein(a) is formed in the liver and transports fats and other lipids such as cholesterol around the body. Lipoprotein(a) is a strong risk marker for cardiovascular disease. One in five has an increased risk of developing a cardiovascular disease and narrowed aortic valve due to a hereditary high content of lipoprotein(a) in the blood. Elevated lipoprotein(a) contributes to a markedly increased risk of cardiovascular disease. Lp(a) is difficult to lower as it is genetically determined.
